# Maker comment — Product Hunt launch day

**Post within 5 minutes of the launch going live.** PH algorithm weights early maker engagement; visitors landing in the first hour see the maker comment as the second thing on the page (right under the description). It frames the entire conversation.

**Tone: personal, not corporate.** Why you built it (real reason, not pitch deck), what makes it different (concrete, not adjective-soup), one ask. Don't beg for upvotes; PH treats that as cringe.

**Length: ~150-250 words. Don't go past 300.** Comments longer than that lose readers; the engagement signal drops.

---

## Final maker comment (paste-ready, 247 words)

```
Hi Product Hunt 👋

I'm Christian, the solo dev behind Everion Mind.

I built this because I lost my own ID number in three different notes apps
last year, and the third time I had to dig for it — at a bank, on hold,
trying to remember which app had the latest version — I realised I was
treating fleeting thoughts and high-stakes facts the same way. They're not
the same.

Everion Mind is two halves of one second brain:

— A memory feed for fleeting stuff: half-thoughts, voice memos, links you
  meant to read, photos of receipts, the diary line you scribbled at
  midnight. AI categorises after, no folders to set up, capture takes
  under five seconds.

— An encrypted vault for high-stakes stuff: ID numbers, gate codes, policy
  numbers, the alarm panel password, "if I'm not around" notes. AES-256-GCM
  derived from a passphrase only you know. We can't read it. We can't sell
  what we can't read.

You ask either half in plain language. The AI grounds answers ONLY in your
own past entries and cites them — no general-internet trivia.

PWA + Android live today. iOS in the App Store this week. Free forever
("Hobby" tier — your own AI key works). $4.99/mo Starter for hosted AI.
$9.99/mo Pro for the premium models.

Two things I'd love feedback on:

1. The "ask half" — does the citation UX feel honest?
2. The vault concept — would you actually use it for ID numbers?

Reading every comment today. Thank you for being here.
```

## Why each part is there

| Paragraph | Job | What NOT to do |
| --------- | --- | -------------- |
| "I'm Christian, the solo dev behind Everion Mind." | Establish you're a real person, not a marketing team | Don't use "we" — solo means solo |
| "Lost my ID number..." | Origin story — concrete, specific, repeatable | Don't say "I noticed users want..." — that's a pitch deck, not a story |
| "Two halves of one second brain" | The non-obvious framing. Most note apps are one-half | Don't lead with features — lead with the model |
| Memory feed bullet | Speed of capture is the proof point | Don't list 8 capture methods — pick the proof point |
| Encrypted vault bullet | Privacy is the moat against the AI-wrapper crowd | Don't say "military-grade encryption" — engineers will close the tab |
| "AI grounds answers only in your own entries" | Differentiator vs every ChatGPT-wrapper | Don't dunk on competitors — the framing is the dunk |
| Pricing line | Honest about money. Three tiers, all named | Don't bury pricing — burying = "trust me bro" |
| Two-question ask | Inverts the upvote-begging into "help me improve" | Don't ask for upvotes. NEVER |
| "Reading every comment today" | Promise of presence | Don't sign off "let me know what you think 🚀🚀🚀" |

## Pre-launch checklist for this comment

- [ ] Update the date if iOS isn't actually live launch-day
- [ ] Update Hobby/Starter/Pro pricing if anything changed
- [ ] Read it out loud — caves to "filler word" creep, edit ruthlessly
- [ ] Have one trusted reader sanity-check before launch day
- [ ] Save the final version in your launch-day notes doc; don't compose at 00:01 PST

## Day-of usage

1. **00:01 PST**: hunter posts the launch
2. **00:02 PST**: open the PH page, verify it's live
3. **00:04 PST**: paste the maker comment as the very first comment on the launch
4. **00:05 PST**: pin the launch tweet on Twitter with the PH URL
5. **00:06 PST onward**: reply to every PH comment within 30 minutes for the first 6 hours

The maker comment isn't a one-shot. Every reply you write today either reinforces the framing or contradicts it. Re-read this comment before each reply if you're tired.

## Variant — if you self-hunt (lower-leverage fallback)

If no hunter signed up by day 50 and you self-hunt, change the opening:

```
Hi Product Hunt 👋

I'm Christian — I'm both the maker and the hunter today (couldn't get a
hunter to bite, totally fair, this is my first launch).
```

The honest framing helps. PH community respects "I tried to find a hunter, didn't work, here we are" more than a fake third-party hunter.
