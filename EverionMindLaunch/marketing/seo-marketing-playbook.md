## 1. The core strategy

Your app should not be positioned as:

“An AI second brain app.”

That is too abstract, too crowded, and too nerdy.

Your stronger positioning is:

Evara Mind is your private AI memory for real life — documents, expiry dates, family info, reminders, and important things you cannot afford to forget.

That is more emotional, more understandable, and more useful to normal people.

Your marketing should sell this feeling:

“I finally have one calm, private place where my important life information lives — and I can ask it anything.”

Before SEO or paid ads, decide the public name.

You have used/considered:

* Everion Mind
* Evara Mind
* Everion
* Evara

For SEO, this matters a lot. Google’s SEO guidance emphasizes making your site easy for search engines and users to understand, which includes clear page titles, useful snippets, and crawlable content. Brand inconsistency makes that harder.

My recommendation:

Company/long-term brand option: Evara
Tagline: Private AI memory for real life.

Do not use “Everion” and “Evara” interchangeably on the live site. Pick one and make every title, footer, logo, domain, Open Graph image, and ad use the same name.

## 4. The target customer

Do not target “productivity people” first. They already have Notion, Obsidian, Apple Notes, Todoist, and ChatGPT.

Target people with messy life admin pain.

### Best early segments

**Segment 1: Busy adults with admin overload**

They forget renewals, documents, warranties, insurance details, school dates, tax info, and family admin.

Pain: “I know I saved it somewhere, but I cannot remember where.”

**Segment 2: Parents and families**

They manage spouse info, kids’ school details, medical details, IDs, documents, passwords hints, appointments, and expiry dates.

Pain: “I’m the one who has to remember everything.”

**Segment 3: Small business owners**

They need to remember supplier details, licences, employee info, renewals, contracts, tax documents, policies, and operational notes.

Pain: “Everything is scattered between WhatsApp, email, files, and my head.”

**Segment 4: People who already use notes apps but still forget things**

They are not anti-tech. They are frustrated that their current tools store information but do not surface it when needed.

Pain: “My notes app is just a dumping ground.”

## 9. Technical SEO checklist

Your site/app should have:

* Server-rendered or statically generated public pages.
* Unique title and meta description for every public page.
* Proper canonical URLs.
* Sitemap.xml.
* Robots.txt.
* Clean Open Graph images.
* Fast mobile performance.
* Descriptive alt text on images.
* JSON-LD structured data.
* Crawlable text, not only text inside images.
* Clear internal links between homepage, use cases, pricing, privacy, and blog.

Google’s structured data documentation says structured data helps Google understand page content and can enable richer search appearances where supported.

### Core Web Vitals targets

Your landing page must feel fast. Google’s Core Web Vitals measure real-world loading performance, interactivity, and visual stability.

Aim for:

* LCP: under 2.5s
* INP: under 200ms
* CLS: under 0.1

Use Search Console’s Core Web Vitals report to monitor real user data.

4. How does Evara Mind solve that?

## 17. Analytics and tracking

Use GA4 or PostHog. Ideally both if you can keep it clean.

GA4’s recommended/custom events model is designed to track meaningful user actions beyond automatic events, and Google also supports marking important actions as key events/conversions.

### Track these events

**Acquisition events**

```
landing_page_view
pricing_page_view
signup_started
signup_completed
```

**Activation events**

```
first_memory_created
first_document_uploaded
first_reminder_created
first_ai_question_asked
first_ai_answer_viewed
```

**Retention events**

```
memory_created
document_added
reminder_added
ai_question_asked
source_opened
weekly_active_user
```

**Monetization events**

```
pricing_viewed
checkout_started
subscription_started
subscription_cancelled
subscription_renewed
```

### Your north-star metric

For the first stage:

Number of users who add 5+ memories and ask 3+ questions within 7 days.

That tells you whether people are actually building a memory, not just signing up.

## 22. What not to do

Do not:

* Build a generic AI blog.
* Use too much “second brain” language.
* Market to productivity nerds only.
* Hide privacy/security pages.
* Send traffic to a vague landing page.
* Optimize for clicks instead of activated users.
* Add too many features before the core memory loop works.
* Change the brand name repeatedly after launch.
* Make users configure too much before they see value.
