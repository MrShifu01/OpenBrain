## How to use this doc

- Each row gets columns: **Who**, **Where**, **Why them**, **First touch**, **Follow-up**, **Status**.
- Status values: `researched`, `pitched`, `replied-yes`, `replied-no`, `published`, `dead`.
- Outreach goes in waves. Wave 1 = friendly contacts. Wave 2 = warm intros. Wave 3 = cold outreach.

## Pitch templates

### Cold email (journalist)

Subject: `Solo SA founder built a privacy-first AI memory app — encrypted, runs on your own data`

> Hi {name},
>
> I've been a fan of your coverage of {specific piece they wrote — actually read it}. I think you might be interested in what I've been building.
>
> Evara is a private AI memory app. You capture life-admin (license expiries, insurance docs, family info, etc.), and it answers your questions in plain English. The differentiator: vault content is end-to-end encrypted before it leaves the device, so even I can't read it. It's been built solo in South Africa over the past year.
>
> Public launch is {date}. I'd love to give you early access if it'd help with a story — happy to walk you through it on a call, or you can sign in here: {invite link}.
>
> Press kit: {URL}
>
> Best,
> Christian

Length: 110 words. No padding. No "I hope this finds you well."

### Newsletter pitch

Same template but with a specific angle that fits their audience. For Lenny: solo activation funnel. For Stratechery: AI consumer infra. For an SA outlet: SA-built tech sovereignty story.

## Tracking

Single Google Sheet (or Notion DB). Don't try to be clever — just need to know what's been said to whom and when to follow up.

## Notes

- Don't mass-email. Each pitch should feel hand-written.
- Don't follow up more than once. Twice is annoying. Three times is desperate.
- If they say "no thanks," reply "Thanks — would love to keep you on the list for future updates if you'd like." (Often turns into a yes 6 months later.)
- Track every "yes" piece of coverage in `Brand/coverage.md` (TODO create when first piece lands).
