## One-paragraph description

> Evara is a private AI memory app for life admin. It remembers your driver's license expiry, where you filed that insurance policy, what your daughter's allergies are — anything you'd otherwise forget. You can ask it questions in plain English ("when does my passport expire?") and it answers from the things you've told it. Works alone or shared with family or a small business team. Encrypted by default. Built solo by Christian Stander in South Africa.

## Two-paragraph description

> Evara is a private AI memory app for the small but constant pile of things you can't afford to forget — license expiries, insurance documents, family medical info, account numbers, the bookkeeper's email, the part number for the boiler. You capture memories by typing, pasting, sharing from email, or speaking. Evara enriches them with AI and makes them retrievable by asking plain-English questions. Reminders fire on dates that matter. Sensitive items go into an end-to-end encrypted vault. Memories can be kept private to you or shared into family or business "brains."
>
> The product is built around a privacy-first architecture: vault content is encrypted in the browser before it leaves the device, meaning even the team operating the service cannot read it. Evara was built solo over 2025–2026 by Christian Stander, a South African engineer, and is currently in beta. It runs on Google's Gemini models by default, with bring-your-own-key support for OpenAI, Anthropic, and OpenRouter on paid tiers.

## Founder bio

> **Christian Stander** is the founder and sole engineer of Evara. He's based in Vereeniging, South Africa. Before Evara he ran restaurants and built tooling for hospitality businesses. He started Evara after watching his family repeatedly miss expiry dates, lose policy documents, and forget important information that wasn't anyone's fault — just life with too many moving pieces. He believes private AI memory should be a default tool, not a luxury, and that the small operator running a family or a small business deserves the same caliber of personal infrastructure that big enterprises buy.

## Talking points (for interview prep)

When asked "why this product?":
> "Because the people in my life kept missing things they cared about. Not laziness — life is just full. The category solves it for big companies; nobody had built it for individuals and families in a way that treated their data with the privacy it deserves."

When asked "how is this different from Notion / Apple Notes / ChatGPT?":
> "Notion is a workspace — you go to it. Apple Notes is unstructured. ChatGPT forgets you between sessions. Evara is the memory under all of them: it watches your life admin, holds onto what you tell it, and answers when you ask."

When asked "what about privacy?":
> "Vault content is encrypted before it leaves your device. We literally cannot read it. For non-vault entries, we send them to AI providers (Gemini by default) under terms that say 'no training, no retention.' We disclose the full data flow at /privacy/ai. If you don't trust us — bring your own AI provider key, and we never see the raw model traffic."

When asked "biggest challenge so far?":
> "Honestly: making the empty state useful. The product gets magical at 30 memories — you start asking questions and it answers from your own life. The first 30 minutes after signup is where most people drop, and where most of the work has gone."
