## What this means

**Eager first-paint cost (gzip):** ~270 KB across the modulepreload set: `rolldown-runtime`, `react-dom`, `jsx-runtime`, `preload-helper`, `LoadingScreen`, `aiSettings`, `authFetch`, `usageTracker`, `supabase` (×2), `sentry`. That's the React + Supabase + Sentry + auth-bootstrap baseline — within the budget of "Lighthouse mobile ≥ 90 perf" once gzip + brotli are applied at the edge. Sentry being preloaded is intentional: ErrorBoundary needs `Sentry.captureException` ready before any error fires; the consent gate stops `Sentry.init()` (network reporting) but the SDK bytes load either way.

**Heaviest chunks are correctly lazy.** exceljs (256 KB gzip!) and pdfjs (121 KB) only load when the user actually interacts with a spreadsheet or PDF. A user who never uploads either never pays for them.

**Vite's "chunks larger than 500 kB" warning** trips on `exceljs.min` (930 KB raw, 256 KB gzip). It's a known vendor library — the warning is informational, not actionable. Acceptable because the chunk is fully lazy.

## Things to do something about

1. **`Everion` chunk at 74 KB gzip** — top contributor to first paint. Will shrink naturally when the three god-components flagged in the readiness audit (TodoCalendarTab, VaultView, EntryList) are extracted into smaller modules. Don't refactor pre-emptively.
2. **`lib` chunk at 98 KB gzip** — biggest non-vendor chunk and growing. Audit candidates for lazy-loading: anything in `src/lib/` that's only used inside one view. e.g., `nlpParser`, `chatContext`, `enrichEntry` are already split out — check `searchIndex`, `entryRepo`, `crypto`, `friendlyError` for whether they could move to per-view chunks.
3. **`focus-trap-react` at 9 KB gzip** — fine, but worth knowing it's pulled in eagerly because CaptureSheet imports it at module top. If first-paint shrinks elsewhere, this becomes a candidate to shave.
