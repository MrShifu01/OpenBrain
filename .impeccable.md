## Design Context

### Users

Broad public audience capturing thoughts, ideas, and knowledge on the go. **Mobile-first** — the primary interaction is a quick capture moment (typing a thought, pasting a link, logging a note while away from a desk). Secondary use is review and organization at a desk. Users trust Everion with their private thoughts and intellectual output, so the interface must feel secure, calm, and worthy of that trust.

### Brand Personality

**Calm. Intelligent. Trusted.**

- **Calm**: No visual noise. Generous whitespace. Nothing fights for attention.
- **Intelligent**: Typography-led hierarchy. Information is clear and well-organized without being clinical.
- **Trusted**: Consistent, predictable, no gimmicks. Feels like a tool that will still be here in 5 years.
- **Focused**: The user's _content_ is the hero — the interface recedes. Minimal chrome.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial minimalism with warm depth** — think a beautifully typeset journal or a refined productivity tool, not a tech product screaming about AI.

**References**:

- **Notion**: Clean, content-first, generous whitespace, confident typography, nothing decorative that isn't functional
- **Obsidian**: Respects the user's intelligence, dark mode that feels warm not cyber, knowledge-first

**Anti-references** (explicitly avoid):

- ❌ Cyan + purple neon on black (the current palette — classic AI slop)
- ❌ Glassmorphism (blur cards, frosted panels)
- ❌ Gradient text on headings or metrics
- ❌ Glow text / glow shadows / synapse backgrounds
- ❌ Bounce/spring easing (the `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` feel)
- ❌ "Claude Code frontend design" look — cold, monospace-forward, tool-aesthetic
- ❌ Blob/orb ambient backgrounds
- ❌ AI product purple-to-blue gradients

**New Direction**:

- **Color**: Warm neutrals — deep warm charcoal (not pure black), off-white cream surfaces, a single restrained accent in warm amber or muted sage — something human and grounded, not synthetic
- **Typography**: A distinctive, editorial display font paired with a refined sans-serif body. NOT Inter, NOT Manrope. Consider: Instrument Serif or Lora for display (trustworthy, editorial), DM Sans or Geist for body (clean, modern)
- **Motion**: Subtle, smooth ease-out. Nothing bounces. Entrances are gentle fades and slides — not performances
- **Surfaces**: Flat or very subtly textured. No glass. Hierarchy through spacing and typography, not shadows and glow
- **Dark mode**: Warm dark — think aged paper inverted. Deep warm brown-grey, not pure obsidian black with neon. Like `oklch(14% 0.01 60)` — slightly warm

### Design Principles

1. **Content is the hero** — interface chrome should nearly disappear; the user's thoughts take center stage
2. **Warmth over tech** — colors, fonts, and spacing should feel human and trustworthy, not synthetic or futuristic
3. **One accent, used sparingly** — a single warm accent color, used only for primary actions and key states; everything else is neutral
4. **Mobile capture first** — every interactive element is thumb-reachable; touch targets are generous; quick capture is the most prominent affordance
5. **Stillness as confidence** — animations are calm and purposeful, never decorative; a still interface signals stability and trust

### Accessibility

- **Target**: WCAG AA compliance
- Minimum contrast ratios: 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators
- Touch targets minimum 44×44px
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored — all animations have zero-motion fallbacks
- Forms use 16px+ font on mobile to prevent iOS auto-zoom

### Brand Mark

- Favicon and logo **must align with the warm amber palette** — the legacy purple/cyan gradient is deprecated
- Brand mark should use warm charcoal + amber accent; no purple, no cyan, no gradient text
- Logo is not a separate system — it shares the app's design language

### Onboarding

- **First-time feel: Instant clarity** — zero friction, one obvious action immediately visible
- No splash screen, no forced tutorial, no multi-step wizard
- Empty state communicates purpose without hand-holding; a single prominent capture affordance does the work
- If onboarding copy is needed, it is minimal, warm, and disappears once the user acts

### Landing Page

- Uses **same design system** as the app — identical tokens, typography, spacing scale, and components
- May use larger display type and hero-scale whitespace, but palette, fonts, and motion rules are identical
- No separate "marketing mode" — Everion's calm, editorial aesthetic IS the pitch
