What is Everion?

Last updated: 2026-04-29 · 8 min read

Everion is a personal second brain plus encrypted vault. One private place for everything worth remembering — fleeting stuff (notes, links, voice memos, half-thoughts, PDFs, screenshots) and high-stakes stuff (gate codes, policy numbers, ID and bank details, where the spare key is hidden, when the licence renews). Capture is one tap. Recall is a chat. Local-first, end-to-end encrypted.

What it does

Everion holds two things people normally keep in separate tools: the half-thoughts you'd lose and the facts your life depends on. Both live in the same atomic entry — a single captured item with no folder, no template, no required title. Type, talk, paste, snap. The AI reads what you've kept and answers questions about it with citations.

Capture

One input surface across mobile and desktop. Text, voice, paste, file upload, photo. No folder picker, no tag prompt, no template. The structure happens later, automatically.

Recall

Ask in plain language. The AI runs retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) over your encrypted entries, finds what matches in meaning, and cites the source so you can verify. Examples that work:

  • "When does my driver's licence expire?"
  • "What did the customer push back on last quarter?"
  • "Where did I hide the spare key?"
  • "What's the gate code at Mom's place?"
  • "What was the policy number for the car?"

Vault

High-stakes facts (bank details, ID numbers, medical info, "if I die" notes) live in an end-to-end encrypted vault. Local-first storage means the data lives on your device first, syncs only when you've authorized it. On the Hobby tier, you bring your own AI key — your prompts go directly to your provider, never through Everion's servers.

Who it's for

Three priority audiences, with a broader household tail once they're in.

  • Founders — high context-switching, lots of half-formed thoughts about product/customers/strategy, plus admin (registrations, tax numbers, supplier banking, contractor details) scattered across email and notes apps.
  • Knowledge workers (PMs, consultants, strategists, analysts) — meeting notes, research, decisions, plus the personal-admin layer (insurance renewals, medical aid numbers, "what's the alarm panel password").
  • Developers — code snippets, architecture sketches, debugging notes, plus the household-CTO role (family accounts, devices, serial numbers, warranties).
  • Household keepers — gate codes, medical info, "if something happens to me" notes for spouse / executor. The single searchable vault the family can find without you.

Frequently asked questions

What is a second brain?

A second brain is a private, searchable place outside your head where you keep the things worth remembering — notes, ideas, links, voice memos, half-formed thoughts, and high-stakes facts like gate codes, policy numbers, or where the spare key is hidden. The point is to stop trying to remember everything yourself. Capture is fast. Retrieval is by meaning, not by where you filed it.

How is Everion different from Notion or Obsidian?

Notion and Obsidian are blank canvases. The system is the work — folders, templates, tags, databases. Most people quit during onboarding. Everion has nothing to set up. One input field, no folders, no templates. The same atomic entry holds a half-thought or a serial number. Retrieval is by asking, not by hunting through a hierarchy you designed last year.

How is Everion different from Mem.ai or Reflect?

Mem and Reflect are AI-native notes apps. Everion covers the same ground for fleeting thoughts but adds a vault layer for high-stakes facts (bank details, gate codes, IDs) with end-to-end encryption — territory those tools don't cover. Everion is also local-first; Mem and Reflect are cloud-first.

How is Everion different from 1Password or Bitwarden?

1Password and Bitwarden are built for credentials — usernames, passwords, 2FA codes. Everion is for the free-form facts that aren't credentials: gate codes, medical aid numbers, account details, "if I die" notes for your spouse. Use both. They don't overlap.

Is my data private with bank details and IDs in Everion?

Storage is local-first — entries live on your device, encrypted. The vault layer uses end-to-end encryption for high-stakes content. On the free Hobby tier you bring your own AI key, so prompts go directly to your provider with no Everion server in the path. Nothing leaves your device until you say so.

What does 'recall by asking' mean?

You don't search by keyword — you ask a question. "When does my driver's licence expire" works. "What did the customer push back on last quarter" works. "Where did I hide the spare key" works. The AI reads your encrypted entries, finds what matches in meaning, and cites the source so you can verify.

Can I share my brain with my spouse or partner?

Yes — on the Pro tier, you can share one brain with one other person. The household / next-of-kin angle: the policy number, the gate code, the alarm panel password, the "if something happens to me" notes are findable by your spouse without you having to be there to answer the question.

Does Everion work offline?

Yes. Everion is a local-first PWA — entries are stored on your device and capture works without a network connection. Sync resumes when you're back online. AI recall requires a network call to your chosen model.

What does 'BYO AI key' mean?

Bring Your Own key. On the free Hobby tier, you supply an API key from Anthropic, OpenAI, OpenRouter, or Groq, and Everion calls those providers directly using your key. Your usage shows up on your provider bill, your prompts never touch our infrastructure, and there's no upsell pressure — the free tier is genuinely usable forever.
