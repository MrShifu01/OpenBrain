Everion vs Mem.ai

Last updated: 2026-04-29 · 5 min read

Short answer: Mem is a cloud-first AI-native notes app focused on fleeting thoughts. Everion covers the same fleeting-thoughts ground but is local-first, has an end-to-end encrypted vault for high-stakes facts (gate codes, policy numbers, IDs, "if I die" notes), and offers a BYO AI key option on the free tier so prompts never touch our servers.

Where Mem wins

  • Mature product surface. Mem has been shipping AI-native notes longer and the surface is polished.
  • Pure cloud experience. If you don't care about local-first or BYO key, the cloud-first model is simpler.
  • Team plans. Mem has team workspaces; Everion's sharing is one-to-one only.

Where Everion wins

  • Privacy posture. Local-first storage, end-to-end encrypted vault, BYO AI key. Storing a bank detail or an ID number in Everion does not require trusting a cloud company with the plaintext.
  • The vault layer. Mem holds notes; Everion holds notes and the high-stakes facts your life runs on (gate codes, policy numbers, medical info, "if I die" notes for your spouse).
  • BYO AI key. Free tier is genuinely usable forever. Your usage shows up on your provider bill, no upsell pressure.
  • Offline-first. Capture works on a flight or in a basement.
  • Shared brain with one other person. The household / next-of-kin angle — Mem's team plans are shaped for work, not for the spouse who needs the policy number.
  • Honest pricing. Free tier is unlimited entries. Paid tiers are flat monthly with no AI usage gotchas — you know what you're paying.

If your priority is...

  • Capture-and-recall for fleeting thoughts only: either tool works. Mem is more mature; Everion is more private.
  • Storing high-stakes facts (IDs, gate codes, bank details): Everion. Mem isn't built for this; the privacy posture is wrong.
  • Bringing your own AI key to keep prompts private: Everion. Mem doesn't offer this.
  • Working offline: Everion. Local-first PWA with proper offline capture.
  • Sharing with a spouse or partner so they can find the policy number: Everion's shared brain on Pro.

Pricing comparison

Both have free tiers. Everion's free Hobby tier is unlimited entries with BYO AI key — you supply your own provider key and pay them directly. Mem's free tier is limited and paid tiers include AI in the price.

FAQ

How is Everion different from Mem.ai?

Mem is an AI-native notes app — cloud-first, focused on fleeting thoughts and quick capture. Everion covers the same ground for notes but adds an end-to-end encrypted vault for high-stakes facts (bank details, gate codes, IDs, "if I die" notes), local-first storage, and a BYO AI key option that keeps prompts off our servers. Mem doesn't have a vault layer.

Is Mem.ai end-to-end encrypted?

No. Mem encrypts data at rest and in transit but is not end-to-end encrypted. Content is processed on Mem's servers for AI features. Everion is local-first with an end-to-end encrypted vault and a BYO AI key option that bypasses our infrastructure entirely on the free tier.

Does Mem.ai let me bring my own AI key?

No. Mem uses its own AI infrastructure and you pay for AI as part of the subscription. Everion's free Hobby tier is BYO AI key — you supply an Anthropic, OpenAI, OpenRouter, or Groq key and pay your provider directly. Your prompts never touch Everion.

Can I store passwords in Mem or Everion?

Use a dedicated password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, Proton Pass) for credentials. Both Mem and Everion are for free-form notes and facts, not for usernames and passwords.

Can I migrate from Mem to Everion?

Export from Mem (Markdown) and import into Everion. Most users start fresh and let the corpus build over time — Everion's value is in capturing what comes up day-to-day, not in importing a back catalogue.
