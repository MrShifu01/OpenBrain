Everion vs Notion

Last updated: 2026-04-29 · 6 min read

Short answer: Notion is a customizable knowledge base for teams who want to build a wiki. Everion is a personal second brain plus encrypted vault for one person (or two on Pro) who wants to capture quickly, recall by asking, and keep high-stakes facts somewhere safe. They solve different problems and most people will use both. This page explains where each one wins.

Where Notion wins

  • Team knowledge bases. Multiple people editing structured content with permissions, comments, and history.
  • Project management. Databases, kanban boards, calendars, dependencies — Everion doesn't do any of this.
  • Customizable structure. If you actually enjoy designing schemas and you'll keep them updated, Notion gives you the canvas.
  • Embeds and integrations. Notion has a deep integration ecosystem; Everion is intentionally narrow.
  • Public publishing. Notion pages can be published as websites. Everion is for things you keep private.

Where Everion wins

  • Capture in under 5 seconds. No folder picker, no template, no title required. The friction kills most second-brain efforts in Notion; Everion's input is one field.
  • Recall by asking. "When does my driver's licence expire" works. The AI reads your entries, finds what matches in meaning, cites the source.
  • High-stakes facts. Bank details, ID numbers, gate codes, medical info — the encrypted vault is built for them. Storing these in Notion is technically possible and practically uncomfortable.
  • Local-first and offline. Entries live on your device first. Capture works on a flight, on a flaky cell connection, on a remote farm.
  • BYO AI key. On the free tier, your prompts go directly to your provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, OpenRouter, Groq). Nothing routes through Everion.
  • Shared brain with one other person. The household / next-of-kin angle — your spouse can find the policy number without you.
  • No system to maintain. Connections happen automatically. No database design. No folder hierarchy that ages badly.

Should I use both?

Yes — most Everion users keep Notion for team work and use Everion for the personal layer underneath. Notion holds the project plan; Everion holds the half-thought you had about the project at 11pm, the gate code at the office, the supplier's banking details, and the policy number for your laptop.

Pricing comparison

Both have free tiers. Everion's free tier is unlimited entries with BYO AI key (you supply your own API key from Anthropic, OpenAI, OpenRouter, or Groq). Notion's free tier is unlimited blocks with limited file uploads and no AI. Notion AI is a paid add-on. Everion's hosted-AI tiers (Starter and Pro) include AI in the price.

FAQ

Should I use Notion or Everion for personal notes?

Use Notion if you want a customizable knowledge base or wiki and you're willing to spend time setting up databases, templates, and a folder structure. Use Everion if you want capture in under 5 seconds with no setup, AI recall over your entries, and an encrypted vault for high-stakes facts (gate codes, policy numbers, IDs).

Can Notion store sensitive information securely?

Notion encrypts data at rest and in transit, but it is cloud-first and does not offer end-to-end encryption. Workspace admins and Notion staff can access content under specific conditions. Everion is local-first with an end-to-end encrypted vault for high-stakes content like bank details and IDs.

Does Notion have AI search?

Notion AI is available as a paid add-on and answers questions across your workspace. Everion's chat is included, runs RAG over your encrypted entries, and cites the source entry. On Everion's free Hobby tier you bring your own AI key, so prompts never touch Everion's servers.

Is Notion good for storing passwords and credentials?

No. Use a dedicated password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, Proton Pass) for credentials. Everion is also not for credentials — it's for the free-form facts that aren't credentials (gate codes, account numbers, medical info, "if I die" notes).

Can I migrate from Notion to Everion?

You don't have to migrate. Most users keep Notion for team and project work and add Everion as the personal capture-and-recall layer. Export from Notion (Markdown or HTML) and paste relevant fragments into Everion when useful — but the bigger win is starting fresh and capturing what comes up day-to-day.
